Accounting & VAT in France for American Business Owners

Whether you're an auto-entrepreneur or running a SASU, managing your French accounting correctly is essential — and the French system is quite different from the US. Here's what you need to know about TVA (VAT), hiring a comptable, and your dual reporting obligations.

TVA (French VAT) — The Basics

📊

French VAT Rates

20% — Standard rate (most goods and services)
10% — Reduced (restaurants, renovations, transport)
5.5% — Reduced (food, books, energy)
2.1% — Super-reduced (press, medications)

🆓

Auto-Entrepreneur TVA Exemption

Micro-entrepreneurs are exempt from charging TVA if annual revenue stays under: €36,800 (services) or €91,900 (trade). Add "TVA non applicable – article 293B CGI" to all invoices.

🌐

B2B Exports to the US

Services provided to US businesses (B2B) are generally outside scope of French TVA (reverse charge mechanism). No TVA to charge on US client invoices. This is a significant advantage for consultants billing US clients.

📋

TVA Declaration & Payment

If registered for TVA: file monthly or quarterly returns via impots.gouv.fr. Collect TVA on French clients, pay it to the government, and reclaim TVA paid on business expenses. Net position due monthly/quarterly.

🧑‍💼

Finding a French Accountant (Comptable) for Expats

  • For SASU/SAS/SARL: a comptable (accountant) is legally required for annual accounts. Budget €1,500–€5,000/year depending on complexity.
  • Look for accountants who specialize in dirigeants d'entreprises étrangers (foreign company directors) — they understand the US reporting intersection
  • Directories: Ordre des Experts-Comptables (experts-comptables.fr), Chambre de Commerce
  • English-speaking accountants in France: firms like Ibanista, Connexion Emploi, or search "English-speaking accountant Paris France"
  • For dual US-French compliance (SASU + Form 5471 + US return): you need both a French comptable AND a US expat tax specialist. They must communicate to avoid double-counting.
