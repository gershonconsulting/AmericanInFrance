Americans Living in Europe

~900,000 Americans call Europe home. Whether you're in France, Germany, Spain or the UK — the core challenges are the same: taxes, banking, healthcare and staying connected to the US.

US Citizenship-Based Taxation

The US is one of only two countries (with Eritrea) that taxes based on citizenship, not residency. Wherever you live in Europe, you must file US taxes every year.

🌍

Key Challenges for Americans in Europe

💸

Dual Taxation

Filing US + local country taxes. Using foreign tax credits and tax treaties to avoid double taxation.

🏦

Banking Difficulties

FATCA makes European banks reluctant to serve Americans. Finding FATCA-compliant banks is essential.

🛂

Visa & Residency

Each EU country has different visa paths. Post-Brexit, UK is separate. Long-stay visas vs. EU freedom of movement.

📋

FBAR & FATCA Reporting

Foreign accounts over $10K must be reported. Penalties for non-compliance are severe — up to $10K+/year.

🏥

Healthcare Access

Enrolling in local health systems vs. international insurance. Understanding what Medicare covers abroad (very little).

🏡

Property Ownership

Different rules per country. US tax implications of owning foreign property. Mortgage access as an American expat.
