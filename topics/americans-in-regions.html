Americans in the French Regions — Life Beyond Paris

While Paris hosts the largest American community, France's regions offer extraordinary quality of life — and growing expat communities. From Lyon's culinary scene to Bordeaux's wine country to the Riviera's sunshine, here's your guide to American life outside the capital.

Major Regional Cities for American Expats

🍽️

Lyon — France's Culinary Capital

2nd largest city. World's best food culture. Growing tech scene (La French Tech Lyon). International schools. More affordable than Paris. American community: ~2,000–3,000. Very livable, great transport links. lyonfrance.com →

🍷

Bordeaux — Wine Capital of the World

Vibrant, renovated city with excellent quality of life. Booming startup scene. 2hrs from Paris by TGV. Warm climate. Large expat community including many British and Americans. Cité du Vin and gastronomy culture. bordeaux-tourisme.com →

✈️

Toulouse — Aerospace & Tech Hub

Airbus headquarters. Major aerospace industry. Pink city. Cap Sciences. Growing startup scene. Paul Sabatier University is world-class for engineering. Warm climate, Spanish influence. Emerging American expat community.

🌊

Marseille — Mediterranean Gateway

France's second-largest city. Diverse, energetic, affordable. Significant Mediterranean culture. Growing tech scene around Marseille Innovation. Beaches, seafood, sun. The Calanques are extraordinary.

🌴

Nice & Côte d'Azur

Mediterranean climate, beaches, mountains. Significant international community. High cost of living. Popular retirement destination for Americans. Monaco nearby. International schools available. nicetourisme.com →

🏰

Aix-en-Provence

Elegant university city in Provence. Paul Cézanne's birthplace. Beautiful climate, market culture, excellent food. Strong international student population (AUP study abroad programs). Very livable for professionals and families.

🌊

Brittany & Normandy

Coastal living with strong American historical connections (Normandy D-Day). More affordable, rugged beauty, slower pace. Ferries to England. Growing digital nomad community. The Dordogne (Périgord) is especially popular with American retirees.

⛷️

Grenoble & Alps Region

World-class skiing, outdoor lifestyle, major tech hub (Silicon Alps). STMicroelectronics, Schneider Electric, Hewlett Packard headquarters. Growing international community. University city with excellent research institutions.

💡

Remote Work Has Changed the Game

The pandemic and the rise of remote/hybrid work has made regional France dramatically more accessible for American expats. US-based remote workers and French-employed remote workers are increasingly choosing Lyon, Bordeaux, or Montpellier over Paris — getting far more space, better quality of life, and 30–50% lower housing costs while maintaining their careers and client relationships via TGV or direct flight.
