France has a thriving independent work culture — and American consultants in France have a unique advantage: US market knowledge that French companies desperately need. Here's how to set up legally, bill properly, and build a sustainable practice.

Choosing Your Freelance Structure

⚡

Auto-Entrepreneur / Micro-Entrepreneur

Best for: Starting out, testing the market, under €77,700/year. Simple: register online, pay charges as a % of revenue, minimal admin. Full guide →

🏢

SASU (Single-Person SAS)

Best for: Revenue above €77,700, want corporate structure, lower effective charges at high income levels. More admin (accountant needed), but more flexible. Full guide →

🛡️

Portage Salarial

Best for: Those who want employee benefits (unemployment, healthcare) while working independently. A portage company employs you and handles all admin. Higher cost but zero admin. Popular choice for consultants transitioning from employment.

🤝

Société de Portage Recommandées

Major portage salarial companies: Freelance.com, ITG, Cegelem, Oxalis, Extens. Typically charge 5–12% of revenue. Compare at portagesalarial.fr

Your Unique Value as an American Freelancer in France

American consultants in France command a significant premium because they possess something rare and valuable:

  • Authentic US market knowledge — not research, but lived experience of the American business culture, networks, and consumer behavior
  • Native English — for US market communications, pitch decks, sales materials, website copy
  • Cultural bridge — understanding both French business culture and American expectations, avoiding costly miscommunications
  • US network — direct introductions to US partners, distributors, VCs, and corporate buyers
  • Regulatory navigation — knowledge of US business registration, compliance, employment law, contractual norms

Invoicing French Clients as an American

French B2B invoices must include: your SIRET number, your full legal name/company, client's SIRET, invoice date, detailed description of services, rate, amount excl. VAT, VAT status line ("TVA non applicable…" if exempt), total amount. Use tools like Pennylane, Freebe, or Shine for compliant French invoicing.
