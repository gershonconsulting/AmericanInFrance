US Embassy & Consulates in France — Services for Americans

The US Embassy in Paris and the US Consulates in Bordeaux, Lyon, Marseille, Rennes, and Strasbourg provide essential services to American citizens in France: passport renewals, emergency documents, notarial services, voter registration, and emergency assistance.

Passport Services

  • Passport renewal in France: Book an appointment at fr.usembassy.gov/u-s-citizen-services/passports/
  • Complete Form DS-82 (if current passport is less than 15 years old and was issued when you were 16+) OR DS-11 (for first-time, minor, or older passport)
  • Passport photos: Must meet US passport photo requirements (white background, 2×2 inches / 5×5cm). Not the same as French photos.
  • Fees: $130 (adults renewal), $100 (minor). Additional execution fee of $35 if applying in person. Pay in EUR equivalent at the Embassy.
  • Processing time: 8–12 weeks routine, 5–7 weeks expedited (additional $60 fee). Emergency same-day passports available for documented emergencies.
  • You can also renew by mail from France (DS-82 eligible passports) — send to the Embassy Paris directly
📋

Other Embassy Services for Americans

⚖️

Notarial Services

The Embassy can notarize US documents (affidavits, powers of attorney, acknowledgments, etc.). Appointment required. Fee: $50 per notarial act. This service is separate from French notaire services.

🆕

Birth & Death Registration

Register a birth abroad (Consular Report of Birth Abroad — CRBA), report a death abroad. Appointments required. The CRBA provides US citizenship documentation for children born in France to US citizen parents.

🗳️

Voter Registration & Absentee Voting

The Embassy can help with federal voter registration. Use the Federal Voting Assistance Program (FVAP) online or the Embassy. Voting by absentee ballot is your right as a US citizen abroad. Full voting guide →

📱

STEP — Smart Traveler Enrollment

Register your residence in France at step.state.gov. Free service that allows the Embassy to contact you in emergencies and crises. Strongly recommended for all Americans in France.

📬

Stay Informed: Embassy Alerts & American Citizen Bulletins

Sign up for emergency alerts and American Citizen Bulletins at STEP (step.state.gov). These free emails notify you of security alerts, demonstration advisories, emergency situations, and changes to Embassy services. Essential for all Americans in France.
